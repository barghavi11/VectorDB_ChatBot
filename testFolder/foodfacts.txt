FASCINATING FOOD SCIENCE FACTS

1. Honey never spoils. Archaeologists have found edible honey in ancient Egyptian tombs that's over 3,000 years old.

2. Bananas are berries, but strawberries aren't. Botanically, berries must have seeds inside their flesh.

3. Chocolate was once used as currency by the Aztecs and Maya. Cacao beans were so valuable that counterfeiting them was a serious crime.

4. Carrots were originally purple, not orange. Orange carrots were developed in the Netherlands in the 17th century.

5. The spiciness of chili peppers is measured in Scoville units. Pure capsaicin measures 16 million Scoville units.

6. Apples float in water because they're 25% air. This is why "bobbing for apples" works as a game.

7. Peanuts aren't nuts - they're legumes that grow underground, more closely related to beans and peas.

8. The holes in Swiss cheese (called "eyes") are created by bacteria that release carbon dioxide during the aging process.

9. Lobsters were considered poor people's food until the mid-1800s and were even fed to prisoners so frequently it was considered cruel punishment.

10. Vanilla is the second most expensive spice in the world after saffron, and it comes from orchid pods.

11. The "five-second rule" has some scientific basis - food picks up fewer bacteria if retrieved quickly from clean surfaces.

12. Tomatoes have more genes than humans - about 31,000 compared to our 25,000.

13. Durian fruit is banned from many public places in Southeast Asia due to its incredibly strong smell.
